Bachelor of Science in Midwifery

The Bachelor of Science in Midwifery (BSM) program is designed to prepare students for a career in maternal and child healthcare. The program provides essential training in pregnancy, childbirth, and the postpartum period. Students will gain the knowledge and skills necessary to assist in labor and delivery, provide prenatal and postnatal care, and educate families on maternal health.

Core Subjects:

  • Human Anatomy and Physiology
  • Maternal and Child Health
  • Pharmacology in Midwifery
  • Midwifery Practice and Procedures
  • Obstetrics and Gynecology
  • Neonatal Care
  • Health Education and Counseling

Career Opportunities:

  • Midwife
  • Maternal Health Educator
  • Neonatal Care Specialist
  • Clinical Instructor
  • Public Health Professional
  • Family Planning Consultant

Program Highlights:

The BSM program emphasizes hands-on clinical training through internships and practical experience. Students are exposed to various healthcare settings and have the opportunity to work with experienced professionals in the field of midwifery and maternal health.
